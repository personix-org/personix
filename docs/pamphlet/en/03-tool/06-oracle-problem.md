# The Oracle Problem — Bridging the Digital and Physical Worlds

The term “oracle problem” has in recent times been most often associated with the world of cryptocurrencies, blockchains, and decentralized systems. Generally: how do you ensure that data entering a digital system faithfully corresponds to what actually happened in the physical world? The system itself cannot verify whether someone really harmed someone else, or whether a contract was truly breached — it only sees digital records.

Here we solve this through authorities — entities that stake their reputation on the digital record corresponding to physical reality. The higher the authority's reputation, the more credible the record.
